10 Most Famous Disappearances

A sneak peek: 3 disappearances

1. Theodosia Burr

In 1812, Theodosia Burr, the daughter of former US Vice President, Aaron Burr, was widely known for her disappearance. It has been cited that her disappearance was in relation sto the Bermuda Triangle. She was a passenger on board the Partiot, which was sailing from Charleston, South Carolina, to New York City until it was never heard of again.

2. USS Cyclops

The disappearance of this navy ship resulted in the largest loss of life in the History of the US Navy. The USS Cyclops went missing without any trace of it's crew of 309 around March 4th 1918, after departing the island of Barbados/

3. Navion A16, Bob Corner

Rob Corner sterted from Miami for a trip to Freeport, Bahamas and was flying his own aircraft: The Navion A16, which was said to have been maintained very well.It was recorded that the plane's tank was full and the distance was short, so shortage of fuel was never a problem. In the middle of the flight, Bob contacted the control tower at Miami to check the weather. The control tower reported that there was a major storm ahead, and that he should take a detour. That was the last time Bob could be contacted. US Coast Gaurds tried looking for him, but there was no evidence of the Navion A16.
